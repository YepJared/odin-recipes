Hummus

Description

Hummus is a creamy, flavorful dip that has its roots in Middle Eastern cuisine, made primarily from blended chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, and garlic. Its smooth texture and rich taste make it a versatile accompaniment, perfect for dipping fresh vegetables, spreading on pita bread, or as a component in various dishes. Hummus is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, providing a good source of plant-based protein, fiber, and healthy fats, making it a popular choice among health-conscious eaters.

Over the years, hummus has gained global popularity and inspired countless variations, incorporating ingredients like roasted red peppers, herbs, and spices. Its adaptability allows for endless flavor combinations, catering to different palates and dietary preferences. Whether enjoyed as an appetizer, snack, or part of a larger meal, hummus continues to be celebrated for its wholesome ingredients, satisfying taste, and cultural significance, bridging culinary traditions across the world.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz/340 g dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 6 fl oz/175 mL lemon juice
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed with salt
  • 4 fl oz/110 mL extra-virgin olive oil
  • 5.5 oz/160 g tahini
  • Salt, as needed
  • Paprika, as needed

Steps

  1. Boil the chickpeas in water in a medium pot until tender, 1 to 2 hours. Drain the chickpeas, reserving the cooking liquid.
  2. In a food processor, blend the chickpeas with about 5 fl oz/150 mL of cooking liquid until they become a smooth paste.
  3. Add the lemon juice, garlic, oil, tahini, and salt. Process until well incorporated.
  4. Adjust seasoning and consistency, if necessary. Garnish with paprika. The hummus is ready to serve now, or may be refrigerated for later use.
